YAWNING. Say: “Excuse me, it’s not the company; it’s the stomach.”

YOUNG GENTLEMAN. Always sowing wild oats; he is expected to do so. Astonishment when he doesn’t.

YOUNG LADY. Utter these words with diffidence. All young ladies are pale, frail, and always pure. Prohibit, for their good, every kind of reading, all visits to museums, theaters, and especially to the monkey house at the zoo.

YOUTH. “What a wonderful thing it is!”. Always quote the Italian lines, even if you don’t know what they mean; “O Primavera! Gioventù dell’ anno! O Gioventù! Primavera della vita!“

YVETOT.1 “See Yvetot and die.” (See NAPLES and SEVILLE.)

 

1 Small town twenty miles from Rouen where, in the fourteenth century, freemen assumed the title of “king”. Béranger’s song about one of these kings popularized the notion that the spot was an earthly paradise.
